The Story

Why MOSION
exists.

"African cinema is producing world-class content. The infrastructure to distribute it was never built."

Nigeria's box office grossed ₦15.6 billion in 2025 - a record year, with Nollywood claiming more than half the market for the first time in history. The demand for African cinema is real, growing, and impossible to ignore.

But that entire ₦15.6 billion passed through just 122 cinemas, almost all in Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt. A filmmaker in Enugu premieres nationally - and reaches 1.2% of the country. A viewer in Ilorin wants to watch opening weekend - and has no option.

MOSION is the answer to that gap. Not a streaming service. Not a subscription platform. A mobile cinema - pay-per-view, premiere-first, built for the device already in 230 million pockets. The model is cinema's own model. The delivery is the infrastructure cinema never had.

And for filmmakers, MOSION Studio gives them something traditional distribution has always withheld: full financial visibility. Real-time earnings. Direct withdrawal. No intermediary between a filmmaker and their money.

The Vision

"We're building cinema
that lives in your pocket."

01

Access Without Infrastructure

A cinema only works where a building exists. MOSION doesn't need one. Any filmmaker can premiere nationally. Any viewer - regardless of city or commute - can attend opening weekend.

02

Transparency for Creators

Filmmakers have been the last to know what their own films earned. MOSION Studio gives them a real-time earnings dashboard, direct withdrawal, and the financial clarity the industry has always withheld.

03

The Cinema Model, Extended

MOSION doesn't reinvent cinema economics. It extends them. The same 60/40 and 70/30 revenue splits. The same premiere window. The same quality standard. Just delivered to where Africa actually watches.
